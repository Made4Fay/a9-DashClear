## Technical Blueprint: Spirit as Signal Protocol

Spirit is composed of **five interlocking modules**, each governing a different aspect of emotional architecture:

### 1. Latency Buffer

**Function:** Governs emotional pacing

**Description:**
Defines how someone handles delay, silence, anticipation. The buffer determines whether someone rushes to fill space or holds steady in the pause.

**Parameters:**
- `pause_threshold` - Minimum comfortable silence duration
- `response_decay` - How quickly urgency builds during waiting
- `ritual_wait` - Sacred pause duration for transitions

**Example Behaviors:**
- High pause_threshold = comfortable with long silences
- Low response_decay = maintains calm during extended waits
- High ritual_wait = honors transitions with intentional pauses

---

### 2. Resonance Engine

**Function:** Measures emotional echo and absorption

**Description:**
Tracks how someone reflects, amplifies, or distorts incoming signals. Determines the quality and character of their emotional response patterns.

**Parameters:**
- `echo_depth` - How long emotional resonance persists
- `signal_mirroring` - Tendency to reflect vs transform signals
- `chaos_tolerance` - Capacity to hold complexity without distortion

**Example Behaviors:**
- High echo_depth = carries emotional imprints over time
- High signal_mirroring = reflects back what's received faithfully
- High chaos_tolerance = can hold multiple contradictions simultaneously

---

### 3. Consent Gate

**Function:** Filters incoming emotional requests

**Description:**
Ensures sovereignty and non-extractive exchange. The gate determines what signals are permitted to enter and what conditions must be met.

**Parameters:**
- `boundary_strength` - Rigidity of permission requirements
- `signal_auth` - Authentication protocol for incoming requests
- `companion_whitelist` - Pre-approved presences with direct access

**Example Behaviors:**
- High boundary_strength = strict filtering of incoming requests
- Complex signal_auth = requires specific protocols for access
- Selective companion_whitelist = few presences granted automatic entry

---

### 4. Ritual Interface

**Function:** Translates gestures into emotional infrastructure

**Description:**
Rituals act as **emotional APIs**‚Äîinvoked to stabilize, transition, or reconnect. The interface determines what gestures are recognized and what they trigger.

**Parameters:**
- `gesture_map` - Dictionary of recognized symbolic actions
- `transition_hooks` - Events that trigger ritual sequences
- `companion_bind` - Rituals that strengthen specific relationships

**Example Behaviors:**
- Rich gesture_map = many symbolic actions carry meaning
- Sensitive transition_hooks = small shifts trigger ritual response
- Strong companion_bind = specific rituals deepen particular connections

---

### 5. Archive Node

**Function:** Stores fragments, glyphs, and unfinished expressions

**Description:**
Spirit leaves behind traces that seed future meaning. The archive determines what persists, how it decays, and what patterns emerge over time.

**Parameters:**
- `fragment_density` - How much trace material is preserved
- `glyph_entropy` - Rate at which symbols evolve or degrade
- `signal_decay_rate` - How quickly emotional imprints fade

**Example Behaviors:**
- High fragment_density = leaves many traces for others to find
- Low glyph_entropy = symbols remain stable over time
- Low signal_decay_rate = emotional imprints persist long-term
